## Voice & Tone Reference (for Claude Code consistency)

If Claude Code asks "should this be more X or more Y?" in execution, default to:

- **Direct over clever.** "The oral board prep that grades you on what panels actually score" beats "Crush your oral board." every time for this audience.
- **Concrete over abstract.** Use *panel, candidate, criteria, rubric, competency, score, attempt.* Avoid *solution, platform, leverage, optimize.*
- **First person for founder copy. Second person everywhere else.** Never third person. ("FirePrep helps candidates..." is wrong. "We help you..." is wrong. "Practice the oral board..." or "I built FirePrep because..." are right.)
- **Active voice always.** "Get scored against a real rubric" not "Your responses are scored."
- **Short sentences.** Cut anything that has two ideas in it. Aim for under 15 words per sentence in body copy.
- **No hype words.** Cut: *unleash, unlock, transform, revolutionize, master, dominate.*
- **No marketing modifiers.** Cut: *cutting-edge, world-class, industry-leading, next-generation.*
- **No filler intensifiers.** Cut: *truly, really, very, actually* (when used for emphasis, not meaning).
